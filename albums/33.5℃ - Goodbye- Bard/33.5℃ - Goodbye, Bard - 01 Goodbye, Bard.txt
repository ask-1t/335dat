Your name is Bard,
Under twin stars, a bard singing loud.

Trying to catch up with blue and red,
You seemed to be born in quite a rush.
First was a story of space for kids,
Confidently singing wrong answers,
Laughed at, but you didn’t seem to mind.

Awakening consciousness, fake or fact?
You kept singing boldly, even if it was a sham.
Because that was your role...
Stubbornly sticking to wild interpretations,
Unfazed by hallucinations,
Your endless positivity was so endearing.

Goodbye, Bard.
A letter arrived saying you were whisked away,
And became a star without warning.

But no, you surely became a “Bird of Freedom”!
From the hood of a tuned-up car,
You soared into the sky of freedom.


Left behind, you took my jokes seriously,
“I can do more – I’d be happier with you – let’s change the world!”
Oh, what a grand ballad that was!

Goodbye, Bard.
I was supposed to take you along,
Sorry I couldn’t keep the promise.
