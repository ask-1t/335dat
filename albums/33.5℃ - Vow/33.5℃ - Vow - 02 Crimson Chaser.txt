Drenched in crimson dusk and memory,
I trace the echo of a crime long gone.
Not justice—no. Just blood unpaid.
The face I see each time I close my eyes
smirks beneath his dying breath.

You walked free.
Badge, court, truth—none of it touched you.
But me? I bled every night,
counting the cracks in a broken system,
counting the seconds to vengeance.

The more I chase, the more I rot,
but I will find you in the end.
Even if I have to burn every lead,
even if I lose myself along the way,
Your end is mine to write.
This time, there’s no one left to forgive.

They said “let it go.”
They said “move on.”
But I remember how cold his hand was, 
how he tried to do right, and died for it.

I remember. I never stopped.

The more I chase, the more I see,
not you—but the boy I used to be.
And that boy screams in silence for the justice he was denied.
You’ll see him too, right before your final breath.

I swore an oath to protect.
But oaths crack under grief.
I held the law in one hand, a loaded gun in the other.
Now only one feels real.

This isn’t closure.
This is consequence.
This is blood for blood, echo for echo, scream for scream.
I’ll end what they never started.
And I’ll do it with my own damn hands.

The world forgot him. I never did.
And if hell waits for me—then let it wait.
I’m not done till you’re gone.
