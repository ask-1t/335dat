Fingertips on a rusted chain,
Still I search the night sky for your face.
You still breathing, Lady Justice?
Only the dry wind answers. Always the wind.

These fists have long forgotten what pain was for.
Pages of the law turned to ash, soaked in liquor and smoke.
But still, every morning,
Something in my chest catches fire.

I don’t know anymore, Is this love, or just a ghost?
It’s so small, so faint, But it burns. Same as ever.

“If that’s your dream, then make it happen.”
 you said.

Your voice tore through the dark like a blade.
It kicked down the rusted, locked-up door
where I buried the part of me that once believed.
That map you left―no key, no mercy.
Just a world painted in blue.
You want me to believe again?
…Damn you!

Neon runs in the rain. Crumbling walls. Dead graffiti.
Clenching my fists won’t bring your smile back.
But still, when I call your name,
My heart jumps, like it’s trying to reach you one last time.

I lived like a clown, knife in my pocket,
So they wouldn’t laugh and call me a kid.
But that one time when the light vanished from your eyes,
That I couldn’t bear.
So like a stray dog, bleeding hope into the dark,
I lit a fire. If that’s what they call a soul,
So be it.

Maybe it’s not love. Maybe it’s not a ghost.
Who cares? It burns.
And that’s enough.

“So that’s your dream. Then go on, make it real.”
 you said.

That voice cut straight through the clouds.
It kicked the door to my past wide open,
Left behind a map with no key.
Drawn on it, a world of blue.
You want me to believe again?
…Fine, Damn it!
