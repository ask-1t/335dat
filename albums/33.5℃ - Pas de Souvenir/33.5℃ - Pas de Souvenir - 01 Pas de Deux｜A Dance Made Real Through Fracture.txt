I. Preludio — Born from a Lie

The stage was set, the score prepared, costume in place
I was handed to your arms, a gift without a choice
But this was not a dance
My legs were bound by duty, by what they all expected

That night, you did not cry
Or perhaps, you could not cry
I was much the same
I’d polished every smile, but nothing underneath


II. Duo — The Miracle of Finding Rhythm

In the cradle of your arms, somehow
I could let my weight fall, unafraid
Little by little, I felt the beat
And lies in our fingertips turned real

I too had practiced smiling
I knew how to catch you ― learned by imitation

And somewhere along the way
Were we humming the same tune?

Steps that began as lies
Yet somehow, we found our breath
Broken pieces fit together
That was the dance that bore our love


III. Intermezzo ― Happiness Breaking Apart

Her voice was like a bell
If one note fell away, the song would shatter
I was so afraid of silence
In my raw, childish despair, I wounded you

I could not cry for her
Not until you asked me, “Why won’t you cry?”
I’d learned to smile so well
But weeping was a skill I could never master


IV. Requiem — To the Sound of Their Voices

At the bottom of the stairs, tiny footsteps
Still echo in the hollow of my ear
After the sound of your weeping
I remember laughter, overlapping

In a room that held only shadows
I danced alone
Even when my legs gave out, I didn’t care
Your presence was

The days when they were here have ended
But the dance remains
That breath, that beat, that warmth
Still, somewhere, they accompany us

(Refrain)


V. Epilogue — Still Dancing Somewhere

You are no longer here
Yet somewhere, you are dancing
So I will dance in another hall
With the shadow of you

I could not become the one
Whose shoulder you now lean on
But if your steps are light
I want to believe that is the answer

The memory of that shared breath
Belongs to no one now
But it existed, truly
Our nameless pas de deux
